Shelters Used By Mountaineers

Base Camp
A base camp is a permanent or semi-permanent camp that serves as a home base for mountaineers during a climbing expedition. It is typically located near the base of a mountain, and may have facilities such as tents, kitchens, and medical stations.
Hut
A hut is a small, simple shelter that is often used by mountaineers as a base for climbing or for overnight stays on a mountain. Huts are typically located at high elevations and may have amenities such as beds, cooking facilities, and toilets.
Tent
A tent is a portable shelter made of fabric and supported by poles. It is often used by mountaineers as a temporary shelter while on a climbing trip, and can be set up and taken down quickly. Tents come in a range of sizes and designs, and can be suitable for use in a variety of weather conditions.
Snow Cave
A snow cave is a shelter dug out of snow and ice. It is often used by mountaineers as a temporary shelter while on a climbing trip in areas with heavy snowfall.Snow caves can be constructed quickly and are relatively simple to maintain, but can be cold and uncomfortable to sleep in.
Bivouac
A bivouac (also known as a bivy) is a temporary, lightweight shelter that is often used by mountaineers as a place to sleep while on a climbing trip. Bivouacs are typically small and can be set up quickly, making them suitable for use in emergency situations or for overnight stays on a mountain. They may be made of fabric or other materials, and can be open or enclosed.
